✅ Do's & ❌ Don'ts

A few golden rules that'll make every prompt you write way better

✅ Do these things
🎯
Be specific. The more detail you give, the better the result. Tell it the format, the audience, the tone.
🔄
Follow up & refine. If the first answer isn't quite right, don't give up — say "make it shorter" or "more formal" and it'll adjust.
👀
Always review the output. Copilot is a first draft, not a final answer. Read it before you send or share anything.
💬
Give it context. "I'm writing to a client who complained last week" gives much better results than "write an apology email."
🎨
Ask for options. Try "give me 3 versions" or "suggest 5 subject lines" — you'll find something you like faster.
❌ Avoid these mistakes
🚫
Don't be vague. "Help me with email" won't get you far. Tell it exactly what you need.
🔒
Don't share sensitive data. No passwords, personal client details, or confidential HR information. Treat it like a new colleague — share only what's appropriate.
📋
Don't copy-paste without reading. Copilot can get things wrong or sound slightly off — a quick read takes 30 seconds.
🧠
Don't assume it remembers. Each new chat starts fresh. You may need to re-give context if you start a new conversation.
🙅
Don't give up after one try. The magic is in the back-and-forth. Refine, push back, ask again differently.
